About the Project

Global Health & Mortality Prediction System

Project Objective

This application helps researchers, policymakers, and public health professionals estimate mortality rates and life expectancy using multidimensional global health data. By combining lifestyle, healthcare access, economic, environmental, and nutrition indicators, the system supports evidence-based health planning and risk assessment.

Machine Learning Models

Two Random Forest Regressor models are trained independently:

  • Model 1: Predicts Death Rate (per 1,000 population)
  • Model 2: Predicts Life Expectancy (years)

Random Forest handles nonlinear relationships among health indicators and provides robust performance on tabular global health datasets.

Dataset Description

The training dataset (data/health_dataset.csv) contains synthetic global health records inspired by WHO and World Bank indicators, including:

  • Demographics (year, gender, population)
  • Lifestyle risk factors (alcohol, tobacco)
  • Healthcare workforce and UHC coverage
  • Environmental and water/sanitation metrics
  • Nutrition and dietary composition
